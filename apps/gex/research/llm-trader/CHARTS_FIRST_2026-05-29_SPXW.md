Yes on the day's one real directional event. **Trade 1 caught the core of the morning breakdown** (the only clean trend of the session), and **Trade 2 caught the early-afternoon range-low break** — both banked as the moves stalled, not chased. ## Lesson for the doctrine

On this day the **negative-gamma "levels break" heuristic was inverted**: the giant 7575 barney and 7600 barney acted as **firm walls/magnets that repeatedly rejected breakdowns**, and the deepening net-negative reading (to −270M) coincided with tighter pinning, not violence. Breakdown-continuation trades that pierced 7575 stalled or reversed at the barney 2 of 3 times. Edge came from fading the *first* clean transition out of a range (T1) and the first range-low break (T2) with fast exits — not from expecting negative gamma to produce follow-through it never delivered.
