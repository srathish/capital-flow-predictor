## 1. Method (pre-registered gate menu)

**ENTRY** = a directional swing signal that ALSO passes a structural confluence gate.
- Signal = swing-ghost zigzag on 1-min closes (R=0.25%, CONFIRM=2, one entry per swing per side, 5-min cooldown): **V-reclaim LONG** (down-swing ≥R off the high, then 2 rising closes) and **rally-reject SHORT** (up-swing ≥R off the low, then 2 falling closes). 42 raw signals across the 3 tickers.
- Gates (computed from the surface at the entry minute):
  - **G1 supportive king** — long: a *real* pika floor (gamma>0, share growing on **5m AND 15m**) within 0.15% below entry; short: a *real* growing resistance node within 0.15% above.
  - **G2 king-flip** — the crown (max |gamma|) migrated ≥0.1% of spot in the trade direction over the last 15 min and is itself growing.
  - **G3 clear path** — nearest strong node in the trade direction is an air-pocket span away (intervening strikes all <3% share) and its distance ≥ 2× the stop distance.
  - **G4 not-fading-velocity** — veto if the *opposing* node's 15-min share Δ ≥ +3pp (don't fade an exploding wall).
  - **G5 regime** — in a +gamma (pin) minute, only fades at range extremes (long in bottom third / short in top third of the day range); in −gamma, breaks allowed anywhere.

**EXIT** (applied to every trade, independent of gates):
- Structural-break stop: close beyond the defining pivot by **0.05% for 1 min** (the validated +4.3% lean).
- Profit-target grid **T ∈ {next-node, +40%, +60%, +80%}** (option-gain targets filled on a limit at the minute's high; next-node fills at the option close when spot reaches the node).
- EOD flat 15:45 ET.
- **Conservative tie-break:** within a minute the stop is resolved *before* the target, so the reported win rate is a lower bound.

Reading the frontier:
- **Deeper targets buy total P&L with win rate.** BASE at +80% books +148% total at only 40% WR (few huge winners, lots of losers); next-node books the *fewest* dollars per winner but the *most* winners. Fast profit-taking lifts hit rate — but here **next-node is strictly better than +40%/+60%/+80% on the G1 set** (73% WR AND +337% total AND −30% worst) because taking the structural node exit forgoes the greedy hold that later round-trips into the stop (that's why G1/+40% has a −57% worst vs next-node's −30%).
- **The "worthless 90%" trap the brief warns about does not appear here — because the structural stop caps losers at ~−30%.** Remove the stop (BASE worst = −81%) and you would get exactly that pathology. On 7/14 no config posts ≥80% WR with a catastrophic loser; the ones that hit 100% do so on **N = 1–3** (below), which is the *other* way a hit rate lies.
- **G4/G5 alone are negative-expectancy.** "Don't fade velocity" and "regime" without G1 keep the wrong trades. They only look sensible *layered on G1*, where they're redundant.

## 5. THE HONEST CAVEAT (read before believing any of this)

**A config tuned on ONE day is a DEMONSTRATION, not a validated edge.** Specifically:

1. **n is tiny and not independent.** 15 trades sounds like a sample; it is not. SPXW/SPY/QQQ are the same exposure engine — the afternoon short cluster (trades #4–#7, #12–#15) is largely *one* market move expressed three times. Effective independent structural events on 7/14 ≈ **5–6**, not 15. A 73% rate on ~6 effective events has a 95% CI that easily spans 40–95%.
2. **The threshold was chosen with hindsight.** I swept 124 configs and picked the one clearing 70%. Some config *had* to clear it by luck alone; the sweep guarantees a survivor. The defense is that the survivor is also the *simplest* (1 gate) and *largest* (N=15) and *mechanistically sensible* — but that is an argument, not proof.
3. **P&L is concentration-driven.** ~50% of the total is 2 trades; one printed on volume=1.
4. **This is a pin/range day.** G1's "real growing floor/cap" gate is tailor-made for a day that pins between nodes. On a trend/gap day it may fire late or fade the move. 7/14 is one regime.

### The exact out-of-sample test that must pass before this means anything
Freeze the config **G1 + next-node + 0.05%/1-min stop, thresholds as in `hitrate_sweep.py` (G1_PROX=0.15%, REAL_GROW=0.15pp on 5m&15m, NODE_MIN_SHARE=5%)** and run it *unchanged* on:

1. **Cross-day OOS.** All other backfill days present (`velocity-capture/backfill/`: 2026-05-21 → 2026-07-13, ~35 sessions × 3 tickers). Report pooled WR, total, expectancy, and **day-block bootstrap** (resample days, P(mean>0)) — reuse `pnl_v0.py::day_block_bootstrap`. Target: WR ≥ 65% and P(mean>0) ≥ 0.9 across ≥ 10 independent days / n ≥ 60.
2. **Random-entry control.** Same ATM contracts, same exit rules, entry minute drawn at random (K=30/contract). G1 must beat the random control's WR and expectancy by a wide, non-overlapping margin. (Within-day, BASE already shows G1 >> ungated; the control must hold *across* days.)
3. **Mirror control.** Flip every signal's direction (long↔short) with the same gates. A real structural edge should *not* survive mirroring; if the mirror is also profitable, the "edge" is just 7/14 drift.
4. **Walk-forward.** Fit any free threshold on days 1..k, test on k+1, roll. No threshold may be re-tuned per test day.
5. **Per-regime split.** Tag each day +gamma vs −gamma (net GEX within 1%); G1 is expected to earn its keep on pin days and must be checked (not assumed) on trend days.

Until #1–#5 clear, treat this as: *"on 2026-07-14, a single structural gate would have delivered 73% / +337% — consistent with the hypothesis, unproven as an edge."*
