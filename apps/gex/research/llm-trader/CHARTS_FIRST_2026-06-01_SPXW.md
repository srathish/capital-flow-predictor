trend; it was a strengthening positive-gamma pin whose only edges were mean-reversion fades off the
reigning king. I took the single cleanest one (7600-king rejection, confirmed by price action, not
by GEX alone) and sat out ~3 hours of untradeable chop plus five failed breakouts — including

round-tripped a chaser.

(2) capping the 0DTE winner into the stall instead of holding for a target the surging floor was
clearly defending; (3) recognizing "flat is a position" on a +400M-gamma pin. Grade: A-. The only
thing left on the table was a marginal, floor-defended second short I correctly judged not worth the
round-trip risk. One trade, +33%, no drawdown — the right shape for a pin day.
