## Did selectivity keep me out of chop?

Mostly yes — and where it failed, it was on the *entry*, not the *pass*. I correctly sat out: the 7405 trap, the 7386 trap, the 7391/7394 stall, all four 7343 support tests, the lunch pin, the neutral-gamma coil (13:31–13:55), and the +71M closing pin. On a day with only two tradeable windows, standing down through hours of pin/chop was the right posture.

The one real error: taking the morning long **into a positive-gamma regime**. Selectivity should have vetoed it — the GEX "air pocket" was real on the map but the regime (pins/chop, strengthening +gamma) said the breakout wouldn't travel, and it didn't. The short (with-regime, negative gamma) was the correct expression of the method; the long was the method mis-applied against the regime. Net takeaway: on a positive-gamma chop day, the honest answer was closer to **1 trade (the short) than 2** — the flat was the better position on the long.
