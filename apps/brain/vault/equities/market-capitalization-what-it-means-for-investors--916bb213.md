Market capitalization is the current value of a publicly traded company, based on the total dollar amount that all of its outstanding shares are worth.

### Key Takeaways

- Market capitalization estimates a company's value by multiplying its share price by its outstanding shares.
- Investors use market cap to classify companies as large-, mid-, or small-cap and compare similar businesses.
- Market cap changes with shifts in share price or shares outstanding.
- It reflects what investors are willing to pay, not a company's intrinsic value or acquisition cost.

## What Is Market Capitalization?

 Market capitalization, or market cap, is the current market value of all of a company's outstanding [stock](https://www.investopedia.com/best-stock-screeners-5120586) shares. Market cap is often used to indicate a company's size and worth in comparison to its peers.

Because stock prices change throughout the trading day, a company's market capitalization also changes continuously, reflecting how the market currently values the business.

## How to Calculate Market Cap

 Market capitalization estimates a company's value by extrapolating what the market thinks it's worth for public companies and multiplying the [stock](https://www.investopedia.com/best-stock-screeners-5120586) price by the number of available shares.

 After a company goes public and trades on an exchange, its share price is determined by supply and demand. As market prices move, the market cap becomes a real-time [estimate of the company's value](https://www.investopedia.com/ask/answers/122414/what-difference-between-market-capitalization-and-revenue.asp). The formula for market capitalization is:

 **Market Cap = Current Share Price x Total Number of Shares Outstanding**

A company with 20 million shares selling at $100 per share has a market cap of $2 billion. A second company with a share price of $1,000 but only 10,000 shares outstanding has a market cap of $10 million.

### Important

An [initial public offering (IPO)](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/i/ipo.asp) helps determine a company's first market capitalization. An investment bank employs valuation techniques to derive a company's value and determine how many shares will be offered to the public and at what price.

## Market Cap and Company Size

Public companies are often grouped by market capitalization, which gives investors a quick way to compare businesses of similar size and maturity.

 [Large-cap companies](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/l/large-cap.asp) typically have a market capitalization of [$10 billion or more](https://www.investopedia.com/dallas-cowboys-value-9000-percent-return-and-how-that-compares-to-apple-microsoft-11803236). These are generally well-established businesses with broad market recognition, stable operations, and access to significant financial resources. Many large-cap companies have long operating histories and may pay regular dividends, although this isn't always the case.

 [Mid-cap companies](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/m/midcapstock.asp) generally have a market capitalization between $2 billion and $10 billion. They often represent businesses that are expanding and may offer greater growth potential than large-cap companies, though they also tend to carry more risk.

 [Small-cap companies](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/s/small-cap.asp) typically have a market capitalization between $250 million and $2 billion. These companies often operate in niche markets or emerging industries. While they may offer higher growth potential, they can also experience greater price volatility and business risk than larger companies.

Companies with market capitalizations below $250 million are commonly referred to as micro-cap stocks. Because they are generally smaller and less established, they tend to be the most volatile and speculative of the market-cap categories.

## Market Cap and Digital Currency

 Market capitalization is also used to estimate the value of cryptocurrencies and other digital assets. It's typically calculated by multiplying the current market price of a token or coin by the number of coins currently in circulation. Some analysts also compare this figure with the fully diluted market capitalization, which assumes all authorized or maximum possible tokens have been issued.

## Misconceptions About Market Caps

 Although the term is used to describe a company, market cap doesn't measure a company's equity value. Only a thorough analysis of a company's fundamentals can do that. Shares are often over- or [undervalued](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/u/undervalued.asp) by the market, meaning the market price determines only how much the market is willing to pay for its shares.

 The market cap does not determine the cost of acquiring the company in a merger transaction. A better method of calculating the price of acquiring a business outright is the [enterprise value](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/e/enterprisevalue.asp).

## Explain It Like I'm Five

Market capitalization tells you how much the stock market says a company is worth. If a company has 1 million shares worth $10 each, its market capitalization is $10 million. As the stock price changes, so does the company's market cap.

## What Factors Alter a Company's Market Cap?

Significant changes in the price of a stock and when a company issues or repurchases shares can affect a company's market cap. An investor who exercises a large number of [warrants](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/w/warrant.asp) can also increase the number of shares on the market and negatively affect shareholders in a process known as [dilution](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/d/dilution.asp). Stock splits do not change market capitalization because the increase in shares outstanding is offset by a proportional decrease in share price.

## What Does a High Market Cap Tell You?

A high market cap signifies that the company has a larger market presence. Larger companies may have less growth potential than [startups](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/s/startup.asp), but established companies may be able to secure financing more cheaply, have a more consistent stream of revenue, and capitalize on brand recognition.

## Does Market Cap Affect Stock Price?

Market cap does not affect stock price. Rather, it's calculated by analyzing the stock price and the number of shares issued. Although a [blue-chip](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/b/bluechip.asp) stock may perform better because of organizational efficiency and greater market presence, having a higher market cap doesn't directly impact stock prices.

## What Is the Importance of Market Cap?

Market cap is often used as an analytical baseline, as all other financial metrics must be viewed through this lens. For example, a company could have had twice as much revenue as any other company in the industry. However, if the company's market cap is four times as large, the argument could be made that the company is underperforming.

## The Bottom Line

Market capitalization is a quick way to estimate the size and market value of a publicly traded company. While it's a useful starting point for comparing investments, it doesn't measure a company's intrinsic value or the full cost of acquiring the business. Investors should consider market capitalization alongside other financial metrics when evaluating a company.
